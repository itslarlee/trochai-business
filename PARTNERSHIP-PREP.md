## Partnership Structures — What to know

### 1. Equity Partner (co-founder level)

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Typical equity** | 10-30% for someone joining after product is built and deployed |
| **Vesting** | ALWAYS use vesting: 4-year vest, 1-year cliff. If he leaves after 6 months, he gets 0. |
| **What he should bring** | Consistent time commitment (10-20+ hrs/week), tangible results (clients signed, revenue, strategy) |
| **Your risk** | Giving away equity to someone who doesn't deliver. Vesting protects you. |
| **When it makes sense** | If he's willing to go part-time or eventually full-time on Trochai |

**Key rule**: He didn't build the product. You did. Post-product equity for a business partner is typically 10-20%, NOT 50/50.

### 2. Advisor with Equity

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Typical equity** | 0.5% - 3% (standard for startup advisors) |
| **Vesting** | 2-year vest, no cliff (or 1-year cliff) |
| **Time commitment** | 2-5 hours/month: calls, intros, strategy reviews |
| **What he should bring** | Network intros, strategic advice, MBA-level financial/GTM analysis |
| **When it makes sense** | If he stays full-time at Zuora but helps on the side |

### 3. Sales/Commission Partner

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Typical commission** | 15-25% of first-year revenue from clients he brings in |
| **Recurring commission** | 5-10% of renewal revenue (optional, for ongoing account management) |
| **No equity** | Pure performance-based. He earns only when he closes deals. |
| **When it makes sense** | If you just need someone to sell, not to shape strategy |

### 4. Revenue Share Partner

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Typical split** | 5-15% of total company revenue (rare and risky for you) |
| **When it makes sense** | Almost never at this stage. Avoid this. |
| **Risk** | You bear all costs, he gets a cut of everything. Misaligned incentives. |

### 5. Hybrid: Small Equity + Commission

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Structure** | 3-8% equity (vested) + 15-20% commission on deals he closes |
| **When it makes sense** | Best of both worlds. He has skin in the game (equity) AND performance incentive (commission). |
| **Recommended** | This is likely the best structure for your situation. |

## Negotiation Playbook

### Before the meeting — know your numbers

| Metric | Value | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| Monthly costs (infra) | ~$275/mo | Vercel Pro + Supabase Pro + misc |
| Revenue | $0 (pre-revenue) | Just deployed, no clients yet |
| Solo plan price | $29/mo | Entry tier |
| Pro plan price | $99/mo | Main target |
| Enterprise plan price | $249/mo | Larger agencies |
| Current valuation | Pre-revenue, hard to value | You set the terms since you built it |

### Your negotiation principles

1. **You built everything alone. That has value.** Product, infra, branding, docs, billing — all you. Any partner joining now is joining a built product, not a blank page.

2. **Vesting is non-negotiable.** No one gets equity without a vesting schedule. If they leave in 3 months, you shouldn't lose equity permanently.

3. **Define deliverables.** "Help with strategy" is vague. Define: "Sign 5 paying clients in 3 months" or "Develop a GTM plan with target list of 50 agencies and outreach templates."

4. **Start small, expand later.** Propose a 3-month trial period with a smaller commitment from both sides before locking in a long-term deal.

5. **Put it in writing.** Even between friends. A simple 1-2 page agreement covering: roles, equity/compensation, vesting, time commitment, and exit terms.

### What to say at lunch

**Opening** (after catching up):
> "Mae, la verdad es que me interesa mucho explorar algo juntos. Dejame contarte donde esta Trochai ahorita y que necesito, y despues me encantaria escuchar como te ves vos participando."

**Present Trochai** (2 min pitch):
- Inbox + AI bot for real estate agencies on WhatsApp
- Live and deployed at trochai.com
- First product (Inbox) ready, need to get first clients
- Billing, docs, everything done — just need sales and growth

**Ask him** (most important part):
> "Ahora, contame — cuando decis partnership, como te lo imaginas vos? Que tiempo le podrias dedicar? Porque eso me ayuda a pensar en la mejor estructura."

**Listen first.** Let him propose. Then you can counter.

**If he proposes equity**, respond with:
> "Me parece bien explorar eso. Lo que si quiero es que lo hagamos con vesting, como se hace en startups, para protegernos a ambos. Y que arranquemos con un periodo de prueba de 3 meses para ver como fluye antes de firmar algo grande."

**If he proposes something too aggressive** (like 30%+ equity):
> "Valoro mucho lo que traes a la mesa, pero el producto ya esta construido y deployado. Para alguien que entra en esta etapa, lo estandar es entre 5-15% con vesting. Podemos complementar con comision por cada cliente que traigas."

### Red flags to watch for

- Wants equity without vesting
- Wants a large stake but can only commit "a few hours a month"
- Vague about what exactly he'll do
- Wants equity but doesn't want to sign a written agreement
- Wants to make big product decisions without deep product knowledge

### Green flags

- Willing to accept vesting
- Has specific ideas about WHO to sell to and HOW
- Can commit consistent hours (even if part-time)
- Already thinking about target market segments
- Brings actual contacts/intros, not just ideas
- Okay starting with a trial period

## Your Long-Term Strategy: Path to Passive Income

Your goal: eventually transition to an investor/royalty role with passive income from Trochai.

### The roadmap

```
Phase 1: Founder-operator (NOW)
├── Get first 10-20 paying clients
├── Validate product-market fit
├── Build repeatable sales process
└── Maybe bring in a partner (Mario?) for sales/growth

Phase 2: Build the team (6-18 months)
├── Hire/partner for sales (if Mario works out, he covers this)
├── Hire 1 support/CS person
├── Document ALL processes (sales playbook, onboarding, support)
└── Revenue target: $5-10K MRR

Phase 3: Systematize (18-36 months)
├── Everything runs with documented processes
├── Team handles day-to-day
├── You focus on product vision and big decisions
└── Revenue target: $20-50K MRR

Phase 4: Step back (36+ months)
├── Transition to board/advisory role
├── Passive income via dividends or royalty on revenue
├── OR sell the company for a multiple of ARR (typically 3-8x ARR for SaaS)
└── OR bring in a CEO and keep majority equity
```

### How a partner fits this strategy

A good partner now (Phase 1-2) **accelerates your path to Phase 4** because:
- You can't do sales + product + support alone forever
- A sales partner gets you to revenue faster
- Revenue = ability to hire = ability to step back

But a BAD partnership deal **delays or kills** your path because:
- Too much equity given away = less incentive for you
- Wrong person = you spend time managing them instead of building
- No vesting = partner leaves, keeps equity, you're stuck

### The right structure for your goals

**Recommended: Hybrid (Small equity + commission + vesting)**

- **5-10% equity**, 4-year vesting, 1-year cliff
- **20% commission** on first-year revenue for clients he personally closes
- **Review at 6 months**: if it's working, formalize. If not, part ways cleanly.
- **His path**: if he performs, he could eventually become Head of Sales/COO with additional equity grants

This protects your majority ownership (for future passive income or sale) while giving him real incentive to perform.
