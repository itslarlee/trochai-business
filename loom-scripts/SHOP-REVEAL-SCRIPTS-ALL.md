# Shop Reveal Scripts — All 13 Shops (Stitch-Ready, Soft Tone V5)

**Strategy:** Record **demo + CTA once** (generic stitch base). Record a **personalized intro per shop** (~45-55 sec each, ~100-130 words). Loom Business+AI stitches them together.

**Tone rules (V5 — conversational, stat-justified):**
- **Never tell them what they lacked.** State facts; let them conclude.
- **Frame observations as research findings**, not critiques.
- **One statistic per intro.** The stat is framed as *why I measured this in the mystery shop* — making it the justification for the research, not a bolted-on claim.
- **Cut every word that doesn't sell.** No "hope you're doing well", no "quick context", no feature lists.
- **On-screen overlay:** when the stat drops, show the source + percentage on screen. The script spells it out explicitly per intro, e.g. `(on-screen: NAR — 78%)`.

**Sales structure per intro (the first 45 seconds IS the sale):**
1. **Hook (0-10s)** — specific finding: praise, pattern, or pattern-interrupt. One sentence.
2. **Founder intro + mystery shop observation (10-25s)** — "I'm Leeren Wu, founder of Trochai — we build the AI + automation layer on top of an agency's WhatsApp. I mystery-shopped [agency] last Wednesday, [specific observation by the clock]."
3. **"Why did I measure X?" (25-40s)** — rhetorical setup that makes the stat feel like the *reason* for the research, not a random fact. Format: *"Why did I measure [X] in the mystery shop? Well — per [SOURCE], [STAT], [tie to observation]. (on-screen: [SOURCE] — [PERCENTAGE])"*
4. **"We solve exactly that" (40-50s)** — benefits-first, not features, **plural by default** (most agencies are multi-agent): *"with our AI assistant you can [benefit 1], [benefit 2], [benefit 3], and **your agents** hop in when they are actually needed. Not on the 97% of conversations that, per NAR and Real Geeks, never end up in a sale. (on-screen: NAR + Real Geeks — 97%)"* — the 97% line is the unifying punchline across every Loom.
5. **Stitch tag** — always close with *"Let me show you."* / *"Le muestro."*

**Stat library (use ONE per intro, matched to observation — always say the source out loud).** Full stat library lives in `trochai-videos/VOICEOVER-SCRIPTS.md`:

- **First-responder wins** (speed / silent / slow reply) → "Per the National Association of Realtors, **78% of buyers go with the first agent that responds**."
- **5-minute rule** (slow reply at high ticket / bot loop / senior broker asleep) → "Per the Lead Response Management Study (Harvard Business Review), **replying in 5 min vs 30 min means you're 21x more likely to qualify the lead** — and the industry average response time is 47 hours."
- **After-hours volume** (silent after-hours / owner personally answering late at night) → "Per NAR and Zillow Group, **62% of real estate inquiries arrive outside business hours** — nights, weekends, dawns."
- **Foreign buyers** (language friction / international buyer angle) → "Per Coldwell Banker Costa Rica, **~40% of Costa Rica real estate buyers are foreigners**."
- **Lost leads** (backup: never-contacted / silent agencies) → "Per NAR and Real Geeks, **97% of real estate leads never convert into a sale**."
- **Follow-up gap** (backup: bot-loop / one-touch-and-drop) → "Per SPOTIO, **48% of agents never follow up with a lead a second time** — while 80% of sales need 5+ contacts to close."

All stats are sourced and already used in public-facing Trochai video content — safe to cite on camera.

**Total per Loom:** ~4:45 (personal intro ~50s + generic demo+CTA ~3:45).
