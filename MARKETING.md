## 3 Mistakes to Avoid Before Anything Else

From SaaS Mastery — these waste time, resources, and delay momentum. Avoiding them puts you ahead of 70% of first-time SaaS founders.

### Mistake 1: Targeting Too Broad

> "If you say 'we help any business be more productive', you've already lost. If you target everyone, you reach no one."

Be the tool that solves **one specific problem** for **one specific type of customer**.

**For Trochai:** We solve slow WhatsApp response times for real estate agencies in Costa Rica. Not "businesses." Not "LATAM." Not "any messaging platform." One country, one industry, one channel.

### Mistake 2: Building Before Validating

> "Founders spend 6 months to a year building the perfect product but never talk to customers."

You should have proof of interest — conversations or signups — before investing heavily.

**For Trochai:** We need discovery calls with CR real estate agencies to validate that slow WhatsApp response is truly a burning pain worth paying to solve.

### Mistake 3: Generic Messaging

> "If your website says 'the future of productivity', this means nothing."

Messaging must be a **clear promise with proof** and why it matters to them. Focus on **benefits, not features**.

**Case study:** Invoice Ocean changed headline to "Generate an invoice in less than 30 seconds" → **2.5x conversion rate increase** from that single above-the-fold change.

**For Trochai:** Don't say "AI-powered WhatsApp platform." Say "Responde a cada lead de WhatsApp en segundos, no en horas."

## The 3 Foundational Principles (TK / Unstoppable)

Before you send a cold email, run a campaign, launch a lead magnet, update your website, run ads, or have AI write a blog post — you need to nail these three foundations. They are what makes AI effective: the right inputs produce the right outputs.

> "Even Anthropic and OpenAI — the companies selling you AI — are paying $200K-$400K for human marketers to work WITH the AI on these decisions. AI gives you research. Humans apply judgment."

### The 3 Disciplines of Marketing

| Discipline | What It Is | When It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| **Product Marketing** | ICP, positioning, messaging, competitive differentiation | **Now** — this is the most important for startups |
| **Demand Generation** | Pipeline, leads, conversion to revenue | **Now** — this drives actual growth |
| **Brand Marketing** | Colors, logo, brand identity, awareness | **Later** — no one cares about your brand if no one knows you |

**For startups: focus on Product Marketing + Demand Gen. Brand comes later.**

## Principle 1: Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

> "It's not just about saying 'companies with 50-100 employees in North America in manufacturing.' It's digging deeper."

When done right, the ICP drives three key decisions:

1. **Positioning** — How do we explain ourselves compared to others in the market?
2. **Segmentation** — Which specific part of the market do we focus on?
3. **Competitive Differentiation** — How do we position against competition (including spreadsheets and apathy)?

### The Multiple-ICP Trap

> "One of the biggest pitfalls is trying to go after 2 ICPs. You can't go after multiple ICPs unless you have enough attention, time, budget, and resources to separate out the areas of focus. For each ICP you go after, you need a different manifesto and a different motion."

**Decision for Trochai:** Focus on **one ICP first** — conquer it, then expand. We don't have the resources to run parallel GTM motions yet.

### ICP Definition (Four Dimensions)

| Dimension | What to Define | Trochai Application |
|---|---|---|
| **Firmographics** | Company size, location, tools they use | 3-20 agents, Costa Rica (GAM first), uses WhatsApp Business (basic), maybe a CRM |
| **Buyer Personas** | Who has authority to buy vs. who has influence but not budget | Agency owner/managing broker (authority) vs. top-producing agent (influence) |
| **Pain Points & Desires** | What keeps them up at night, goals, fears, suspicions, who they see as enemy | Losing leads to faster competitors, fear of AI replacing agents, suspicious of tech complexity, enemy = agencies that respond faster |
| **Goals & Motivations** | What they ultimately want to achieve | Close more deals, manage more leads without hiring, look professional to international buyers |

> "When you know exactly who you are talking to, every decision becomes easier. Your messaging, your pricing, your feature prioritization — it all flows from a clear ICP. Your goal is to find the person who is most likely to need, want, and buy your product."

### Primary ICP — Real Estate Agency Owner/Manager in Costa Rica

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Role** | Agency owner, managing broker, or operations manager |
| **Company size** | 3-20 agents |
| **Location** | Costa Rica (GAM initially, then beach/tourist zones) |
| **Current pain** | Losing leads because agents don't respond fast enough on WhatsApp; no centralized inbox; manual follow-up |
| **Tech maturity** | Uses WhatsApp Business (basic), maybe a CRM. Not deeply technical |
| **Budget** | $50-200/month per agency is reasonable |
| **Trigger events** | Hired new agents, lost a deal due to slow response, peak season approaching |
| **Where they hang out** | CCCBR, Facebook groups for CR real estate, WhatsApp groups, local RE events, Encuentra24 |
| **Greatest competitor** | Not software — it's **apathy** (doing nothing, sticking with manual WhatsApp) |

### Positioning (vs. Competition)

| Competitor | Their Gap | Our Position |
|---|---|---|
| Generic WhatsApp CRMs (Respond.io, Wati) | Not built for real estate, no property AI, no Spanish-first | Domain-specific: we understand properties, not just messages |
| Local CR CRMs | No WhatsApp AI integration | WhatsApp-native with AI from day one |
| ChatGPT / generic AI | No WhatsApp integration, no property database, no multi-agent inbox | Integrated into the workflow, not a separate tool |
| Manual WhatsApp Business | No automation, no team features, no AI | 10x faster response, zero missed leads |
| **Apathy (doing nothing)** | **This is the biggest competitor** | Every hour you don't respond, a competitor does |

**Our moat:** Deep real estate domain (property search, project matching, listing context) + WhatsApp-native + Costa Rica market fit.

## Principle 2: The Manifesto (Strategic Narrative)

> "Another name for this is your strategic narrative. Based on your positioning, segmentation, and competitive differentiation, there are things you have to define."
>
> "Anthropic is hiring a 'Head of Go-to-Market Narrative' paying $300K-$400K. They need someone to figure out the broader message, the strategic narrative, how to differentiate from OpenAI, how to address common objections and fears."

The manifesto has two layers:

### Layer 1: Value Proposition

One-sentence explanation of: **what you do + what problem you solve + why you're 10x better.**

This feeds into every cold email, ad, organic post, and landing page.

**Trochai Value Proposition (draft — test and iterate):**

> "Trochai responds to every WhatsApp lead in seconds with AI that understands your properties — so you never lose a deal to a slow response again."

**Variations to test:**

| # | Value Proposition | Angle |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | "Responde a cada lead de WhatsApp en segundos, no en horas" | Speed/time contrast |
| 2 | "Tu agente de ventas en WhatsApp que nunca duerme" | 24/7 availability |
| 3 | "No pierdas otro cliente por no responder a tiempo" | Fear of loss |
| 4 | "Atiende 10x más leads sin contratar más agentes" | Scale without hiring |
| 5 | "Conecta tu WhatsApp en 5 minutos. Tu agente de IA empieza a responder hoy." | Speed to value |

### Layer 2: Messaging & Strategic Narrative

How the value proposition is **deployed across key marketing assets**: homepage, outbound email subject lines, ads, lead magnets, social posts.

**Core message framework:**

| Element | Message |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | Real estate agencies in Costa Rica lose deals because leads on WhatsApp go unanswered for hours. The average agency takes 4+ hours to respond. By then, the lead has moved on. |
| **Villain** | Time. Every minute that passes, the chance of closing drops. Your competitors are faster. |
| **Solution** | Trochai is an AI-powered WhatsApp inbox that responds instantly, 24/7, with knowledge of your property listings |
| **Differentiation** | Built specifically for Costa Rica real estate — understands properties, projects, zones, prices, Spanish, and local context |
| **Proof** | (Build with first customers — response time metrics, conversion improvements, testimonials) |
| **Objection handling** | Security: WhatsApp Business API compliant. AI quality: human handoff built-in. Complexity: setup in 5 minutes. |
| **CTA** | "Connect your WhatsApp in 5 minutes. Your AI agent starts responding today." |

> **Key insight from TK:** AI will always give you an answer for your manifesto. It will never say "I don't know" or "we have headwinds" or "this is a strategic decision we should talk through." This is where human judgment matters most. The manifesto is a set of **choices** that only the founder can make.

## Principle 3: The Broadway Show (Execution Framework)

> "One of the biggest mistakes I've seen early stage companies make is they get scatterbrained. TikTok yesterday, LinkedIn today, Twitter tomorrow. Marketing only works when you run in 3-week iterations."

### What Is the Broadway Show?

A **consistent set of sales and marketing activities** that you:
1. Create once
2. Run for a **3-week period**
3. Collect data and draw insights
4. If it works → **scale it**
5. If it doesn't → **iterate and run again**

> "You create it once and you run it for a three week period. If it works, you scale it. If it doesn't, you iterate and run it again."

### The Stacking Order

Each layer builds on the previous. Start at the top, prove it works, then stack the next.

```
1. ORGANIC SOCIAL          ← Test messaging here first (free, fast data)
   ↓ if it works
2. PAID SOCIAL             ← Scale what works organically (pay for reach)
   ↓ as paid runs
3. OUTBOUND                ← Messaging is proven; they'll see your paid social too (dual attack)
   ↓ once outbound runs
4. SEM / SEO / EVENTS      ← Longer tail, less data, but stacks on everything above
```

> "If it worked in organic, it's going to work in paid. You don't have to become a content creator or influencer. You test organically, prove the messaging works, then pay the platform to send more leads."

### Why This Order Matters

- **Organic first:** Free to test. Gets you data on what messaging resonates. If headlines don't work organically, they won't work paid.
- **Paid second:** Once messaging is proven, you pay to scale. You stop being a content creator — just scale ad spend.
- **Outbound third:** By now messaging is battle-tested AND your outbound recipients will see your paid social → **dual-flag attack** = trust.
- **SEM/SEO/Events last:** Longer to build, slower data. But by now you know your keywords, your messaging, your ICP.

**These all stack.** Once someone meets you at an event, they'll see your paid social, find your content via SEO, and get your outbound email. You're everywhere.

### Core Metric: Pipeline → Revenue

> "We don't need to do brand marketing. What we really focus on is demand generation marketing, which means pipeline, not likes. We want actual leads that are converting to revenue."

**Don't optimize for vanity metrics** (likes, impressions, followers). Optimize for:
1. **Traffic** — How much attention are we getting?
2. **Conversion** — How many become leads?
3. **Activation** — How many take the next step (book call, start trial)?
4. **Traffic Quality** — What % of leads are actual ICP leads?

### Marketing vs. Sales

> "Marketing is one-to-many (infinitely scalable). Sales is one-to-one. Outbound emails have a 1% response rate. The better the marketing, the easier the sale — because trust is built before the call."

Once the Broadway Show works with paid social converting to real meetings/pipeline/trials, you can **infinitely scale** it.

## Trochai's Broadway Show Plan

### Iteration 1 (Weeks 1-3): Organic Social — Test Messaging

**Platform:** Facebook + Instagram (where CR real estate agents live; LinkedIn secondary)

**What to post (3x/week minimum):**
- Pain-point posts: "¿Cuánto tarda tu agencia en responder un mensaje de WhatsApp?" with poll
- Tip posts: "3 formas de no perder leads en WhatsApp" (value-first, no product pitch)
- Before/after: "Lo que ve tu cliente cuando tarda 4 horas vs. 30 segundos"
- Behind-the-scenes: Building Trochai, what we're learning from CR agencies
- Social proof (once available): Customer quotes, metrics

**Measure after 3 weeks:**
- Which posts get engagement from ICP (not random people)?
- Which messaging angles resonate? (Speed? Fear of loss? Scale?)
- Any DMs or conversations started?
- Click-through to landing page?

### Iteration 2 (Weeks 4-6): Paid Social — Scale What Works

Take the best-performing organic posts and turn them into ads.

**Key setup:**
- Target: CR real estate professionals on Facebook/Instagram
- Use lead magnets (see below) to capture leads
- **Only fire conversion pixel for ICP leads** — trains the algorithm to send more ICP traffic
- Track traffic quality (% of leads that match ICP)

**Benchmarks from TK's case studies:**
- 20-49% conversion rate on organic content
- 14-22% conversion rate on paid
- 50-80% traffic quality (ICP leads)
- 15-53% activation rate

### Iteration 3 (Weeks 7-9): Outbound — Proven Messaging + Dual Attack

Now layer in outbound with battle-tested messaging. Prospects who receive outbound will also see paid social ads → trust multiplier.

**Outreach tactics (from Margot / SaaS Mastery):**

#### Tactic A: Social Engagement (Warm Before You Pitch)
1. Find agency owners on Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn
2. Follow them, engage with property posts, leave thoughtful comments for 1-2 weeks
3. Then reach out with feedback angle: "I've built something for people like you and I'd love your feedback"

#### Tactic B: Lead with Value
- **WhatsApp audit report:** "I tested your agency's WhatsApp response time. Here's what I found."
- **Competitor analysis:** "Here's what the top 3 agencies in Escazú are doing differently"
- **Tips document:** "5 ways to convert more WhatsApp leads into property viewings"

#### Tactic C: Personalized Loom Videos (Highest Response Rate)
1. Record a 4-5 min personalized video per prospect
2. Show their website/Instagram on screen, use their name
3. Demo Trochai responding to a WhatsApp message about their properties
4. **Pro tip — Gossip Effect:** Send to two decision makers at the same agency, mention you also sent to their colleague
5. **Pro tip — Collaborate, don't criticize:** Never say "this is bad." Use "yes, and..."

**Outreach cadence per prospect:**
- Day 1: Personalized WhatsApp message or Loom
- Day 3: Follow-up with a specific insight about their business
- Day 7: Share a relevant case study or tip
- Day 14: Final follow-up, offer free trial

### Iteration 4+ (Weeks 10+): SEM, SEO, Events

**SEM (Google Ads):**
- Target high-intent keywords validated by what worked in paid social
- "WhatsApp CRM inmobiliaria Costa Rica", "automatizar WhatsApp bienes raíces"

**SEO (Blog content on trochai.com/blog):**
- Long-form articles targeting the same keywords that convert in SEM
- "WhatsApp para inmobiliarias Costa Rica"
- "Cómo automatizar WhatsApp para bienes raíces"
- "Cómo responder más rápido a leads de WhatsApp"

**Events:**
- CCCBR events, local RE conferences
- By now you have messaging, social proof, paid social running → when they meet you once, you're everywhere after

## Phase 0: First 10 Customers (Unscalable, Conversation-Driven)

> "The first 10 customers are the hardest because you have to do things that are not scalable."

**At this stage, your main task is to have conversations.** You don't sell — you uncover challenges. The currency is feedback + payment. These first 10 become the input for your ICP definition and your manifesto.

### Trochai First 10 Playbook

1. **Personal network:** Reach out to anyone connected to CR real estate
2. **WhatsApp groups:** Join CR real estate agent groups, participate genuinely
3. **CCCBR contacts:** Attend events, meet agency owners face-to-face
4. **Demo on the spot:** "Send a WhatsApp to this number and watch what happens"
5. **Offer free pilot:** In exchange for feedback + testimonial commitment

> **Key insight:** These first 10 help you understand what they have in common — that becomes your ICP. Their words about their pain become your messaging. Their results become your proof.

## Customer Feedback Loop

> "This is the secret skill that separates good founders from great ones. Obsess over feedback."

**After each customer onboard:**

1. **Week 1:** Daily check-in. Is the bot responding correctly? Edge cases?
2. **Week 2:** Weekly check-in. What's missing? What's working?
3. **Month 1:** Monthly review. Share their metrics. Ask for testimonial.
4. **Ongoing:** WhatsApp group with early customers for feedback + community

**What to track per customer:**
- Messages handled by AI vs. human
- Average response time (before vs. after Trochai)
- Lead conversion rate change
- Feature requests and pain points
- Willingness to refer others (NPS)
- Quotes and stories usable as testimonials

> Feedback improves the product AND improves your messaging. What customers say about their pain becomes your best marketing copy.

## Phase 2: Scaling Beyond 100 Customers

### Success Stories Library

> "Use storytelling: 'One of our clients in your industry was struggling with the exact same problem. Here's what they did and here's what happened.' — This has way more impact than 'our client got to XYZ revenue.'"

- Publish 3-5 case studies with real metrics
- Frame as stories: "Agency X in Escazú was losing 3/5 WhatsApp leads because they took 4+ hours to respond. After Trochai, response time dropped to 30 seconds. In month one, 12 more property viewings booked."
- Short, authentic video testimonials from agency owners

### Referral Program

> "Build it into your onboarding process. Automated message after a few months: 'Love our product? Share it with a friend and you both get [reward].'"

- Automated WhatsApp message after 2-3 months of usage
- Reward: free month, credits, or feature unlock
- Make it frictionless (one-click referral link)

### Thought Leadership & Content

> "Position yourself so clients come to you. Content should solve ICP's pain points, not just talk about your product. This brings down CAC in the long run."

- Blog, YouTube, Instagram Reels, TikTok — all solving ICP problems
- Topics: WhatsApp best practices for RE, lead response benchmarks, automation tips
- This is the long-term brand play that makes paid acquisition optional over time

### Pricing Optimization

> "As you scale, look at your pricing so you can scale profitably. This determines how much you can spend on marketing per customer."

- Review pricing tiers based on actual usage data from early customers
- Ensure unit economics support paid acquisition (LTV > 3x CAC)

## Using AI the Right Way

> "There's a right way and a wrong way to use AI for marketing. Even Anthropic and OpenAI — selling you their AI — hire humans at $200K-$400K to work WITH the AI."

### The Human-AI Loop

| Phase | AI's Role (Research) | Human's Role (Judgment) |
|---|---|---|
| **ICP** | Research markets, competitors, demographics, customer data | Make strategic choices on positioning, segmentation, differentiation |
| **Manifesto** | Draft messaging variants, analyze competitor copy, suggest angles | Choose the strategic narrative, decide what to emphasize, apply taste |
| **Broadway Show** | Write content, analyze metrics, optimize ads, generate variations | Draw insights from data, decide what to iterate, apply wisdom on what's working and why |

> "AI has to be research. The human has to be judgment. You can't one-shot this. Give AI a new piece of information and it gives you a completely different answer. This is why you need experienced judgment to direct the AI."

### What AI Won't Do

- Tell you "I don't know the answer"
- Say "we have real headwinds and we need to figure out the product piece first"
- Flag concerns or present strategic tradeoffs proactively
- Apply taste, wisdom, or market intuition

**Use AI for:** research, content generation, data analysis, A/B variant creation, metrics tracking
**Use human judgment for:** ICP choices, messaging strategy, interpreting what metrics mean, deciding when to iterate vs. scale
