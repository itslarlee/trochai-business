Five data points crossed my feed this month worth lining up:

- 53% of CR SMEs advancing rapidly in digital transformation, 78% on the journey (Microsoft 2023, via DPL News)
- 50% of CR SMEs already using AI in daily ops — 62% for customer service, 48% to improve client satisfaction (Microsoft Source LATAM 2025)
- CR ranks 5th in LATAM in AI readiness, score 53.83/100 (ILIA 2025; top 5: Chile, Brazil, Uruguay, Colombia, CR)
- 92.6% internet penetration / 4.78M users (DataReportal Digital 2026)
- PropTech 2026 in CR: virtual tours, AI for price prediction, asset management (The Costa Rica News, Dec 2025)

Read individually, they're five stories of digital optimism. Read together, something's missing — none of them name real estate. Hospitality yes. Banking yes. Retail yes. Manufacturing yes. But the sector that moves hundreds of millions in CR per year isn't in the picture.

My honest take, open to correction by data: adopting AI isn't the architectural problem. It's a derivative. The architectural question is whether inventory — the catalog, prices, availability — lives in one place or scattered across surfaces that don't talk. Add AI on top of fragmentation and you get generic answers. Add it on top of a single source of truth and modest tools start producing disproportionate results.

The 2026 question isn't "do I need AI?" It's "is my system in shape for AI to add value?" The first one's a feature debate. The second one's architecture.
