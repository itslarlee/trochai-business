Did 20+ discovery calls with real estate agency owners in Costa Rica between Feb and April. Same question every time, slightly reworded: "How many properties on your website have already been sold but are still listed as available?"

The most common answer wasn't a number. It was silence. Or "probably five. Maybe ten." Or "haven't checked in about a month."

One person — out of 20+ — said "zero, I checked yesterday." She managed her inventory in one place.

The rest managed it in five+ (MLS, portals, site, WhatsApp, Instagram). And here's the part that changed how I pitch: this isn't a discipline problem. It's a math problem. Staying in sync across five disconnected places isn't a habit you build. It's an equation that doesn't solve.

Clients notice the drift in 8 seconds — they ask via WhatsApp about a property they saw on the site, the bot says "no longer available", the site says it is. Trust is gone before the human agent finds out.

The fix starts the day inventory has one owner. The other four surfaces just read from it.
