# Trochai LinkedIn-First Content Strategy

LinkedIn as primary channel. Daily posting with a visual (carousel/infographic) on every post. Founder-led, educational content. Instagram as light secondary (2-3 cross-posts/week).

---

## Why LinkedIn First

- **Your ICP is there.** Real estate agency owners and managers making software decisions are on LinkedIn, not watching TikToks. They're vetting vendors, forming opinions, making purchasing decisions.
- **The field is empty.** 1.3 billion members, but only 3% post weekly. Showing up daily puts you ahead of 97% of the platform.
- **The algorithm favors small accounts now.** LinkedIn's 360 Brew update (2025) actively gives better distribution to smaller accounts with quality content. A great post from a 5-10K account outperforms an average post from a 50K account.
- **Educational content = Trochai's strength.** Data-driven frameworks, stat-backed insights, step-by-step guides — this is what wins on LinkedIn and what Trochai already produces.
- **B2B buying happens here.** When someone sees your content and thinks "I need this", they can act the same day. Different buying environment than any other platform.

## The Algorithm: 360 Brew (2025+)

LinkedIn replaced its entire ranking system with a single AI model called 360 Brew — 150 billion parameters, trained on LinkedIn's own data.

**What changed:**
- Old system matched keywords. New system reads **meaning**.
- Old system needed you to follow/engage. New system checks **relevance** to the individual.
- One AI brain replaced the committee of modules (trending, hashtags, engagement velocity).

**What it rewards:**
- **Relevance** — content matching the reader's profile, experience, interests
- **Dwell time** — people actually reading, not just scrolling past
- **Saves** — LinkedIn now prioritizes saves over likes (create saveable content)
- **Depth** — longer, deeper content outperforms shallow posts
- **Genuine engagement** — real comments with substance, not "great post"
- **Hooks** — first 1-2 sentences get **3-5x more processing attention** from the algorithm

**What stopped working:**
- Timing optimization and hashtag strategies
- Engagement pods (360 Brew detects fake engagement)
- Generic advice (thousands posting the same tips = competing in same lane)
- Short, shallow posts for quick likes

**What this means for Trochai:**
- Every post needs a strong hook in the first 1-2 lines
- Create content people save (frameworks, checklists, data)
- Go deep — longer carousels, more slides, more detail
- Stay within 3 core topics (algorithm builds a content fingerprint of you)

## Profile Optimization

Your profile is a landing page. Your content is the ad. If the ad is great but the landing page is confusing, nobody converts.

### Leeren's LinkedIn Profile Structure

**Headline** (not job title — value proposition with keywords):
```
Building Trochai — AI-powered WhatsApp inbox for real estate | Helping CR agencies respond to every lead in <2 min | PropTech founder
```
Every element is intentional: keywords buyers search for, the platform, what you do.

**Banner:**
- Visual billboard — rehook after the headline
- Should make visitors think "I need to keep reading"
- Include: what Trochai does, who it's for, one visual metric

**About section** (Problem → Solution → Proof → CTA):
1. Problem your ICP faces (lead response time, after-hours gaps)
2. What Trochai does about it (AI WhatsApp automation)
3. Numbers/proof (stats, early results)
4. CTA: newsletter link, booking link, or trochai.com

**Featured section:**
- LinkedIn Newsletter subscription
- Lead magnet (WhatsApp Response Grader, templates PDF)
- Booking link for demos
- Priority: newsletter > lead magnet > booking (move people into your ecosystem)

**Custom CTA button:**
- Link to newsletter signup or demo booking page

**Services section:**
- Fill with exact language your ICP uses: "WhatsApp automation", "AI chatbot for real estate", "lead management for agencies"

### LinkedIn SEO

360 Brew reads your profile before distributing your content. Your profile tells the algorithm what topics you're qualified on.

- **Headline:** Use keywords, not just titles (e.g., "AI WhatsApp automation for real estate" > "Founder at Trochai")
- **About:** Natural keywords matching ICP search language
- **Services:** Searchable — most people skip this, fill it in
- **Post consistently on core topics:** Algorithm builds a content fingerprint over time
- **Name carousel files** descriptively before uploading (tells the system what the document is about)
- **Add alt text** to images (the system reads it)

## Content Buckets (40/30/20/10)

Every piece of content has a job. Four buckets, four jobs.

### Growth Content (40% — ~3 posts/week)

Expands reach to people who don't know you yet. Borrows attention from things people already care about.

**Formats:**

- **Brandjacking:** Take a well-known brand (Zillow, Compass, Redfin, Airbnb), a decision they made, and use it as a frame for your own insight. You're not reporting — you're borrowing their name recognition. "Airbnb just changed their pricing algorithm. Here's what it means for CR agencies."
- **Newsjacking:** When something significant happens in your industry, be the first credible voice to contextualize it. Sharp take within 24-48 hours of breaking news. "Costa Rica just changed the property tax rules. Here's what no one is saying."
- **Namejacking:** Reference a specific person your audience follows. Use their idea as a launchpad for your own perspective. "Mike DelPrete says proptech is consolidating. Here's what that means for small agencies in Latin America."
- **Hot takes:** A contrarian angle on something the industry accepts as true. Forces people to take a position → engagement velocity → distribution. "Agencies don't need more leads. They need to answer the ones they already have."

### Authority Content (30% — ~2 posts/week)

Builds trust. Turns "interesting opinions" into "this person can actually help me."

**Formats:**
- Frameworks and step-by-step guides (carousel format)
- Data-backed analysis (infographics with stats)
- Case studies and results breakdowns
- Deep-dive educational content

**Key:** Educational content branded properly is more memorable, more saveable, and more valuable than personal content. Package your knowledge into visual, actionable frameworks.

### Conversion Content (20% — ~1-2 posts/week)

Creates pipeline. Asks for the business after trust is built.

**Formats:**
- Product demos (screen recordings, before/after)
- Customer results and testimonials
- "How Trochai works" walkthroughs
- Specific use cases ("Here's what happens when a lead writes at 2AM")

Conversion posts rarely go viral. That's fine — they convert the people already in your funnel.

### Personal Content (10% — ~1 post/week)

Makes you memorable. The human behind the expertise.

**Guidelines:**
- Keep it generic and relatable — "building a SaaS", "lessons from launching", "what I learned this week"
- Don't fabricate personal stories or overshare
- Tie it back to real estate tech or the startup journey
- "Building in public" angle: what you shipped, what broke, what surprised you

## Hooks Strategy

The first 1-2 sentences get 3-5x more processing attention from 360 Brew. If the hook doesn't stop the scroll, nothing else matters.

### What a Good Hook Does

1. **Stops the scroll** — curiosity, bold claim, or pattern interrupt
2. **Builds credibility** — specific numbers are more compelling than vague claims
3. **Signals relevance** — call out the ICP: "Directores de agencias inmobiliarias..."
4. **Gets the "See more" click** — everything on LinkedIn lives behind that click

### Hook Templates

| Type | Template | Example |
|------|----------|---------|
| **Stat shock** | "[Stat]% de [group] [surprising behavior]." | "97% de los leads inmobiliarios se pierden. Y no es por el precio." |
| **Contrarian** | "Todo el mundo dice [common belief]. Están equivocados." | "Todo el mundo dice que necesita más leads. Están equivocados." |
| **Question** | "¿[question that triggers self-reflection]?" | "¿Cuántos leads perdió su agencia este fin de semana?" |
| **Bold claim** | "[Strong statement] sin [expected requirement]." | "Cerramos un deal a las 2 de la mañana sin que ningún agente estuviera despierto." |
| **ICP callout** | "[Job title]: [something they care about]." | "Directores de agencias: su tiempo de respuesta le está costando dinero." |
| **Story open** | "[Time/place]. [Unexpected situation]." | "2:17 AM. Un comprador de Toronto preguntó por un apartamento en Escazú." |

## Comment Flywheel (First 90 Days)

When you post to zero followers, almost nobody sees it. But a strong comment on a post hitting 50,000 impressions puts you on the busiest street in town.

### How It Works

1. **Build an engagement list** — Spreadsheet with 20-30 accounts whose audience matches your ICP
   - Columns: Category, Name, Activity URL (NOT profile URL — they can see profile visits)
   - Focus on 3-10K follower accounts with strong engagement from your ICP, not 100K vanity accounts
2. **30 minutes daily** before posting — leave substantive comments on their posts
3. **A good comment** does one of three things:
   - Adds a new angle the post didn't cover
   - Respectfully challenges the take with data
   - Tells a quick story that connects to the topic
4. **Reply to every comment** on your own posts, especially in the first hour
5. **"Great post" is not a comment.** Nobody reads it and it does nothing for you.

### Timeline

- First 20 posts: Practice. Finding your voice. Don't expect traction.
- Posts 21-40: You start finding what resonates. Voice sharpens.
- Posts 41+: Momentum kicks in. Don't quit before you get here.

## Pre-Validating Content

Don't guess what will work. Study what already works, then make your own version.

### How to Pre-Validate

1. **Find outliers** — posts that got 5-10x more engagement than the creator's average
2. **Study similar ICP accounts** — 3-10K followers in proptech, real estate, B2B SaaS
3. **Analyze what made it work:**
   - What was the hook?
   - What format (carousel, video, text)?
   - What topic?
   - How did they structure the content?
4. **Adapt, don't copy** — take the structure/format, apply your own expertise and data
5. **Focus on small accounts** — big accounts get natural lift, small account outliers = the content is the hero

### Steal Formats, Not Ideas

- Take a hook structure that worked → rebuild with your own data
- Take a framework someone used for marketing → apply to real estate
- Take a carousel design that got saves → fill with Trochai's insights

This isn't stealing — it's how all content works. James Clear didn't invent habits. He branded them better.

## Instagram (Light Secondary)

Instagram stays as a secondary channel. Don't create separate content — cross-post from LinkedIn.

**What to cross-post (2-3/week):**
- LinkedIn carousels → Instagram carousels (same PNGs)
- Video scripts → Instagram Reels (rendered via Remotion)
- Newsletter carousel → Instagram carousel

**What NOT to do:**
- Create Instagram-specific content
- Maintain a separate Instagram content calendar
- Spend time on Instagram Stories, trending audio, or platform-specific tactics
- Post to TikTok (dropped for now)

**Why keep Instagram at all:**
- LATAM real estate culture is very visual / Instagram-heavy
- Remotion infrastructure is already built
- Cross-posting is nearly free (same assets, different captions)

## Video Editing Tools

### Recommended Stack

| Tool | Use | Cost |
|------|-----|------|

### Video Production Rules

- Vertical (9:16) for Reels
- Captions on every video (85% watched on mute)
- First 3 seconds = hook (no intros, no logos)
- 15-45 seconds sweet spot
- Natural lighting, phone camera is fine
- Spanish (ustedeo) primary
